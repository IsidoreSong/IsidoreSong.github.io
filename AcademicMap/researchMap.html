The Cognitive Bridge — Pondering the Essence of Intelligence

Core Insight: Redefining "Intelligence"

Inspired by my own "internal contradictions" and "self-reflection," I realized: The hallmark of a truly intelligent system should not be flawless, unidirectional reasoning, but the ability to handle internal conflicts, engage in self-examination, and perform iterative correction. This is the essence of human "reflection" and "hesitation," and it is the cornerstone for achieving higher-level intelligence.

Future Research — Integrating Multimodal Perception and Latent Space Reasoning

Future Direction Core Objective Connection to Past Work Research Path & Methods Key Paper References
1. Multimodal Alignment Construct a multimodal conceptual space that supports reasoning: Move beyond surface-level feature matching to build a unified latent space where multimodal representations are not only aligned but also possess the structure and compositionality required for complex logical reasoning. The experience of handling heterogeneous unimodal data in the QGFace project provides a methodological foundation for addressing the deeper conceptual heterogeneity across different modalities.
  • Deep Conceptual Alignment: Investigate how to align abstract concepts, attributes, and relationships across modalities (e.g., "dynamic motion" in an image with "running" in text) to form a structured representation of world knowledge.
  • Building a Composable Latent Space: Design alignment strategies that enable compositionality in the latent representations, where real-world concepts emerge from a combination of information across multiple modalities.
  • Foundation: CLIP, Flamingo
  • Structured Concepts: PaLM-E, Graph Representation Learning, VinVL
2. Latent Space Reasoning Reconstruct latent reasoning structures to endow models with internal thought capabilities: The core goal is to design and implement a new internal computational flow that enables models to perform autonomous, iterative, and correctable "thinking," rather than simply compressing external reasoning processes into the model. The epiphany from the 'Cognitive Bridge'—that AI needs the ability for 'self-examination and iterative correction'—directly motivates this research direction to redesign the model's internal reasoning structure and grant it genuine thinking capabilities.
  • From Explicit Reasoning to Implicit Thinking: Explore how to transform external, symbolic reasoning chains (like CoT) into an internal, continuous, and dynamic computational flow, making it an intrinsic capability of the model.
  • Design Endogenous Reasoning Architectures: Build models with recurrent and dynamic computational capabilities (e.g., vertical/horizontal recurrence) that can engage in "deliberation" based on problem complexity, moving beyond a fixed feed-forward pass.
  • Starting Point: wei2022cot
  • Architecture Design: Universal Transformer, Mamba, Recurrent-Depth
  • Interpretability-Guided Architecture: Circuit 2022, Memory3
